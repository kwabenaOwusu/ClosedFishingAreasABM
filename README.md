
# *Agent-Based Model to Investigate the Effects of Configuration, Timing, and Duration of Closed Fishing Areas on the Ecologic and Economic Sustainability of the Artisanal Sardinella Fishery in Ghana.*

## Purpose 
In an effort to rebuilding the overfished Sardinella stocks in Ghana, the Ministry of Fisheries and Aquaculture Development (MoFAD) and its implementing agency, the Fisheries Commission, have been implementing a closed fishing season for all fishing fleets from 1 July to 31 July for artisanal since 2019. We developed a spatially explicit ABM that captures the main characteristics of the Ghanaian artisanal Sardinella fishery. We then used the model to investigate the spatial configurations of fishery closure, and the duration (for how long it must be in place) of the fishery closure that optimizes, simultaneously, economic returns (in terms of catches) and ecologic benefits (in terms of maintaining the fish abundance stocks to sustainable levels) of the Ghanaian Sardinella artisanal fishery. Specifically, we examine the impacts of three spatial configurations (1) total closure of the IEZ. (2) closure of the ecologically or biologically significant marine area (EBSA) of the IEZ, and (3) no closure of the IEZ. These three spatial configurations are implemented across varying fishing effort regimes and upwelling intensities. We further examined three alternative durations for the proposed closure of the ecologically or biologically significant marine area (EBSA): (1) the first month of the upwelling season (i.e., July), (2) the first two months of the upwelling season (i.e., July–August), and (3) the entire upwelling season (i.e., July–September).
